Main Street
vs.
Wall Street

Our project seeks to answer the question that has been asked by many recently: Do the U.S. equity markets reflect economic reality? The rapid recovery of the stock market despite unemployment rates in the double digits and the slow recovery of the economy has forced us to question whether there is a strong correlation between economic growth and market capitalization for U.S. based companies.

Median Income
By State

As a first step in our endeavor to analyze "Main Street" one of the economic indicators we looked at Median Household Income by state. The median income appears to be high on the east and west coasts, with most Middle America (except Utah and Colorado) at the lower end of this range.

Market Capital
By State

Market capital is an indication of overall corporate wealth. We used a combination of S&P 500 and Nasdaq companies. State-wide distribution of this indicator shows capital concentration trends as being higher on the East Coast with California, Washington, and New York at the higher end.

Unemployment Rate
By State

The Unemployment Rate in 2019 trends were in line with Median Income overall. Unemployment was higher on the East and West Coasts, and lower in the middle states.

2019 Revenue
By State

When looking at state revenue it does appear that there is an unequal distribution in the United States. Four states, California, New York, Texas and Florida alone accounted for 36% of the revenue generated in 2019.
