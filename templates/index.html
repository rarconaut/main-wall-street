Main Street
vs.
Wall Street

Our project seeks to answer the question that has been asked by many recently, do the U.S. equity markets reflect its
economic reality? The recent rapid recovery of the stock market despite unemployment rates in the double digits and the
slow recovery of the economy has forced us to question whether there is a strong correlation between economic growth and stock market returns.

Median Income
By State

As a first step in our endeavor to analyze "Main Street" economic indicators we looked at the median income by state for
the last three years. The median income appears to be high on the east and west coast with most of the fly over states
(except Utah and Colorado) at the lower end of this indicator.

Market Capital
By State

Consumer spending trends continue to be strong due to the low interest rates offered by the Federal Reserve Bank. The
state wide distribution of this indicator follows the median income trends as in the high earning states on the two coasts
were also the high spending ones.

Unemployment Rate
By State

Unemployment rate in 2019 was inverse to the median income and consumer spending as it was higher for the states on
the east and west coasts and lower in the fly over states
in the middle.

2019 Revenue
By State

When looking at state revenue it does not take one long to discover that there is an unequal distribution of it in the United
States. Four states, California, New York, Texas and Florida accounted for 36% of the revenue generated in
2019.
